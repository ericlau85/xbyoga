शं नो मित्रः शं वरुणः । शं नो भवत्वर्यमा ।
शं नो इन्द्रो बृहस्पतिः । शं नो विष्णुरुरुक्रमः ।
नमो ब्रह्मणे । नमस्ते वायो । त्वमेव प्रत्यक्षं ब्रह्मासि ।
त्वामेव प्रत्यक्षं ब्रह्म वदिष्यामि । ॠतं वदिष्यामि ।
सत्यं वदिष्यामि । तन्मामवतु । तद्वक्तारमवतु ।
अवतु माम् । अवतु वक्तारम् ॥
śaṃ no mitraḥ śaṃ varuṇaḥ । śaṃ no bhavatvaryamā ।
śaṃ no indro bṛhaspatiḥ । śaṃ no viṣṇururukramaḥ ।
namo brahmaṇe । namaste vāyo । tvameva pratyakṣaṃ brahmāsi ।
tvāmeva pratyakṣaṃ brahma vadiṣyāmi । ṝtaṃ vadiṣyāmi ।
satyaṃ vadiṣyāmi । tanmāmavatu । tadvaktāramavatu ।
avatu mām । avatu vaktāram ॥
May Mitra grant us peace, and Varuna grant us health;
May Aryaman bring us welfare, and Indra bless our wealth;
May Brihaspati bestow wisdom, and Vishnu's cosmic stride;
Protect our home and kindred, with the deities as our guide.
Homage to Brahman, homage to Vayu, the wind;
Thou art verily the visible Brahman, in thee all truth we find;
I shall declare thee as Brahman manifest, I shall speak the cosmic law;
I shall utter truth eternal, without any flaw;
May that protect me ever, and protect the speaker too;
May it guard me and the teacher, in all we say and do.
भद्रं कर्णेभिः शृणुयाम देवाः । भद्रं पश्येमाक्षभिर्यजत्राः ।
स्थिरैरङ्गैः तुष्टुवाग्ं सस्तनूभिः । व्यशेम देवहितं यदायुः ॥
स्वस्ति न इन्द्रो वृद्धश्रवाः । स्वस्ति नः पूषा विश्ववेदाः ।
स्वस्ति नस्तार्क्ष्यो अरिष्टनेमिः । स्वस्ति नो बृहस्पतिर्दधातु ॥
bhadraṃ karṇebhiḥ śṛṇuyāma devāḥ । bhadraṃ paśyemākṣabhiryajatrāḥ ।
sthirairaṅgaiḥ tuṣṭuvāgṃ sastanūbhiḥ । vyaśema devahitaṃ yadāyuḥ ॥
svasti na indro vṛddhaśravāḥ । svasti naḥ pūṣā viśvavedāḥ ।
svasti nastārkṣyo ariṣṭanemiḥ । svasti no bṛhaspatirdadhātu ॥
May we hear with our ears what is auspicious, O ye worthy of worship!
May we see with our eyes what is auspicious, O ye adorable ones!
May we, strong in limbs and body, singing your praise,
Enjoy the life which the gods have allotted us!
May Indra of great fame be propitious to us!
May Pushan, who knows all beings, be propitious!
May Tarkshya, who harms none, be propitious!
May Brihaspati ensure our welfare!
आप्यायन्तु ममाङ्गानि वाक्प्राणश्चक्षुः श्रोत्रमथो बलमिन्द्रियाणि च सर्वाणि ।
सर्वम् ब्रह्मोपनिषदम् । माऽहं ब्रह्म निराकुर्यां ।
मा मा ब्रह्म निराकरोद निराकरणमस्त्व निराकरणम् मेऽस्तु ।
तदात्मनि निरते य उपनिषत्सु धर्मास्ते मयि सन्तु ते मयि सन्तु ॥
āpyāyantu mamāṅgāni vākprāṇaścakṣuḥ śrotramatho balamindriyāṇi ca sarvāṇi ।
sarvam brahmopaniṣadam । mā'haṃ brahma nirākuryāṃ ।
mā mā brahma nirākaroda nirākaraṇamastva nirākaraṇam me'stu ।
tadātmani nirate ya upaniṣatsu dharmāste mayi santu te mayi santu ॥
May my limbs, speech, prana, eyes, ears and strength
And all my senses ever be nourished and developed!
All is Brahman, as declared in the Upanishads.
I shall never deny Brahman, nor shall Brahman deny me!
Let there be no denial at all! Let there be no denial at least from me!
May those virtues that are prescribed in the Upanishads
Dwell in me, who am devoted to the Atman! Yea, may they dwell in me!
वाङ्मे मनसि प्रतिष्ठिता । मनो मे वाचि प्रतिष्ठितम् ।
आविराविर्म एधि । वेदस्य म आणीस्थः ।
श्रुतं मे मा प्रहासीः । अनेनाधीतेन अहोरात्रान्सन्दधामि ।
ऋतं वदिष्यामि । सत्यं वदिष्यामि ।
तन्मामवतु । तद्वक्तारमवतु ।
अवतु माम् । अवतु वक्तारम् ॥
vāṅme manasi pratiṣṭhitā । mano me vāci pratiṣṭhitam ।
āvirāvirma edhi । vedasya ma āṇīsthaḥ ।
śrutaṃ me mā prahāsīḥ । anenādhītenā । ahorātrānsandadhāmi ।
ṛtaṃ vadiṣyāmi । satyaṃ vadiṣyāmi ।
tanmāmavatu । tadvaktāramavatu ।
avatu mām । avatu vaktāramavatu vaktāram ॥
May my speech be rooted in my mind, and my mind be rooted in my speech!
O Brahman, reveal Thyself to me! Enable me to grasp the Vedas!
May what I have heard not depart from me!
May I spend day and night in studying this!
I shall speak of the cosmic law, I shall speak the truth!
May That protect me, may That protect the teacher!
May That protect me, may That protect the teacher!
नमो ब्रह्मणे नमो अस्त्वग्नये नमः पृथिव्यै नम ओषधीभ्यः ।
नमो वाचे नमो वाचस्पतये नमो विष्णवे बृहते करोमि ॥
namo brahmaṇe namo astvagnaye namaḥ pṛthivyai nama oṣadhībhyaḥ ।
namo vāce namo vācaspataye namo viṣṇave bṛhate karomi ॥
Homage to Brahman! Homage to Agni!
Homage to Earth! Homage to herbs and plants!
Homage to Vak (Speech)! Homage to the Lord of Speech!
Homage to Vishnu the vast! Thus do I offer my salutations!
यश्छन्दसामृषभो विश्वरूपः । छन्दोभ्योऽध्यमृताथ्संबभूव ।
स मेन्द्रो मेधया स्पृणोतु । अमृतस्य देवधारणो भूयासम् ।
शरीरं मे विचर्षणम् । जिह्वा मे मधुमत्तमा ।
कर्णाभ्यां भूरिविश्रुवम् । ब्रह्मणः कोशोऽसि मेधया पिहितः ।
श्रुतं मे गोपाय ॥
yaśchandasāmṛṣabho viśvarūpaḥ । chandobhyo'dhyamṛtāthsaṃbabhūva ।
sa mendro medhayā spṛṇotu । amṛtasya devadhāraṇo bhūyāsam ।
śarīraṃ me vicarṣaṇam 。 jihvā me madhumattamā ।
karṇābhyāṃ bhūriviśruvam । brahmaṇaḥ kośo'si medhayā pihitaḥ ।
śrutaṃ me gopāya ॥
He who is the bull of the metres, of universal form,
Who has sprung from the metres, from immortality,
May that Indra fill me with intelligence!
May I become the vessel of immortality!
May my body be capable of great deeds,
May my tongue be exceedingly sweet!
May I hear abundantly with my ears!
Thou art the sheath of Brahman, hidden by intelligence!
Protect what I have heard!
त्र्यम्बकं यजामहे सुगन्धिं पुष्टिवर्धनम् ।
उर्वारुकमिव बन्धनान् मृत्योर्मुक्षीय मामृतात् ॥
tryambakaṃ yajāmahe sugandhiṃ puṣṭivardhanam ।
urvārukamiva bandhanān mṛtyormukṣīya māmṛtāt ॥
We worship the three-eyed Lord Shiva, who is fragrant and who nourishes all beings.
May He liberate us from death for the sake of immortality,
Even as the cucumber is severed from its bondage to the creeper.
सह नाववतु सह नौ भुनक्तु सह वीर्यं करवावहै ।
तेजस्वि नावधीतमस्तु मा विद्विषावहै ।
saha nāvavatu saha nau bhunaktu saha vīryaṃ karavāvahai ।
tejasvi nāvadhītamastu mā vidviṣāvahai ।
May He protect us both! May He nourish us both!
May we both work together with great energy!
May our study be brilliant! May we never hate each other!
तच्छं योरावृणीमहे । गातुं यज्ञाय । गातुं यज्ञपतये ।
दैवी स्वस्तिरस्तु नः । स्वस्तिर्मानुषेभ्यः ।
ऊर्ध्वं जिगातु भेषजम् । शन्नो अस्तु द्विपदे । शं चतुष्पदे ॥
tacchaṃ yorāvṛṇīmahe । gātuṃ yajñāya । gātuṃ yajñapataye 。
daivī svastirastu naḥ । svastirmānuṣebhyaḥ ।
ūrdhvaṃ jigātu bheṣajam । śanno astu dvipade । śaṃ catuṣpade ॥
For that peace we choose, for the sake of sacrifice,
For the lord of sacrifice, for divine welfare,
For human welfare, upward goes the healing balm,
Peace to the bipeds, peace to the quadrupeds!
अहं वृक्षस्य रेरिवा । कीर्तिः पृष्ठं गिरेरिव ।
ऊर्ध्वपवित्रो वाजिनीव स्वमृतमस्मि ।
द्रविणग्ं सवर्चसम् । सुमेध अमृतोक्षितः ।
इति त्रिशङ्कोर्वेदानुवचनम् ॥
ahaṃ vṛkṣasya reriva । kīrtiḥ pṛṣṭhaṃ gireriva ।
ūrdhvapavitro vājinīva svamṛtamasmi ।
draviṇagṃ savarcasam 。 sumedhā amṛtokṣitaḥ ।
iti triśaṅkorvedānuvacanam ॥
I am like the bird perched on the tree, free and at ease;
My fame is like the mountain peak, reaching to the skies;
Pure as the horse's upright mane, I am the self-born immortal;
Rich in splendor and radiance, blessed with good intelligence,
Anointed with the nectar of immortality.
This is the Vedic recitation of Trishanku.
नमो वाचे या चोदिता या चानुदिता तस्यै वाचे नमो नमो वाचे नमो वाचस्पतये नम ऋषिभ्यो मन्त्रकृद्भ्यो मन्त्रपतिभ्यो मामामृषयो मन्त्रकृतो मन्त्रपतयः परादुर्माऽहमृषीन् मन्त्रकृतो मन्त्रपतीन् परादां वैश्वदेवीं वाचमुद्यासँ शिवामदस्तांजुष्टां देवेभ्यः शर्म मे द्यौः शर्म पृथिवी शर्म विश्वमिदं जगत् ।
शर्म चन्द्रश्च सूर्यश्च शर्म ब्रह्मप्रजापती । भूतं वदिष्ये भुवनं वदिष्ये तेजो वदिष्ये यशो वदिष्ये तपो वदिष्ये ब्रह्म वदिष्ये सत्यं वदिष्ये तस्मा अहमिदमुपस्तरणमुपस्तृण उपस्तरणं मे प्रजायै पशूनां भुयादुपस्तरणमहं प्रजायै पशूनां भूयासं प्राणापानौ मृत्योर्मा पातं प्राणापानौ मा माहासिष्टं मधु मनिष्ये मधु जनिष्ये मधु वक्ष्यामि मधु वदिष्यामि मधुमतीं देवेभ्यो वाचमुद्यासँ शुश्रूषेण्यां मनुष्येभ्यस्तं मा देवा अवन्तु शोभायै पितरोऽनुमदन्तु ॥
namo vāce yā coditā yā cānuditā tasyai vāce namo namo vāce namo vācaspataye nama ṛṣibhyo mantrakṛdbhyo mantrapatibhyo māmāmṛṣayo mantrakṛto mantrapatayaḥ parādurmā'hamṛṣīn mantrakṛto mantrapatīn parādāṃ vaiśvadevīṃ vācamudyāsaṃ śivāmadastāṃjuṣṭāṃ devebhyaḥ śarma me dyauḥ śarma pṛthivī śarma viśvamidaṃ jagat ।
śarma candraśca sūryaśca śarma brahmaprajāpatī । bhūtaṃ vadiṣye bhuvanaṃ vadiṣye tejo vadiṣye yaśo vadiṣye tapo vadiṣye brahma vadiṣye satyaṃ vadiṣye tasmā ahamidamupastaraṇamupastṛṇa upastaraṇaṃ me prajāyai paśūnāṃ bhuyādupastaraṇamahaṃ prajāyai paśūnāṃ bhūyāsaṃ prāṇāpānau mṛtyormā pātaṃ prāṇāpānau mā māhāsiṣṭaṃ madhu maniṣye madhu janiṣye madhu vakṣyāami madhu vadiṣyāmi madhumatīṃ devebhyo vācamudyāsaṃ śuśrūṣeṇyāṃ manuṣyebhyastaṃ mā devā avantu śobhāyai pitaro'numadantu ॥
Homage to Speech, whether expressed or unexpressed!
Homage to the Lord of Speech! Homage to the seers,
The makers of mantras and lords of mantras!
May the seer-makers and mantra-lords not desert me!
I shall not desert the seers, makers and lords of mantras!
I offer this universal divine speech, benign and pleasing to the gods.
Peace to me from heaven, peace from earth, peace to this entire universe!
Peace from the moon and sun, peace from Brahma the creator!
I shall speak of beings, of the world, of energy, of fame,
Of austerity, of Brahman, of Truth!
For that I spread this sacred grass as my seat.
May this seat bring me progeny and cattle!
May I attain progeny and cattle!
May my inhalation and exhalation protect me from death!
May they not desert me!
I shall think of honey, I shall be born as honey,
I shall speak honey, I shall speak sweet words!
I offer this honeyed speech to the gods,
Worthy of being served, to mankind!
May the gods protect this! May the fathers approve it for splendour!

गणानां त्वा गणपतिंहवामहे कविं कवीनामुपमश्रवस्तमम् ।
ज्येष्ठराजं ब्रह्मणां ब्रह्मणस्पत आनःशृण्वन्नूतिभिःसीदसादनम् ॥
ॐ श्री महागणपतये नमः ॥
gaṇānāṃ tvā gaṇapatiṃ havāmahe kaviṃ kavīnāmupamaśravastamam ।
jyeṣṭharājaṃ brahmaṇāṃ brahmaṇaspata ānaḥśṛṇvanūtibhiḥsīdasādanam ॥
oṃ śrī mahāgaṇapatayē namaḥ ॥
We call upon Thee, Lord of Hosts, the Poet amongst poets, of matchless renown;
The supreme sovereign of prayers, Lord of sacred wisdom, hearkening to our invocations with favours, be Thou seated in this place.
Om, adoration to the glorious and great Lord of Hosts.
वक्रतुण्ड महकाय सूर्य कोटि समप्रभ ।
निर्विघ्नं कुरु मे देव सर्व कार्येषु सर्वदा ॥
vakratuṇḍa mahakāya sūrya kōṭi samaprabha ।
nirvighnaṃ kuru mē dēva sarva kāryēṣu sarvadā ॥
O Thou with the curved trunk and mighty body, whose splendour is equal to a crore of suns;
Grant that my undertakings be ever free from obstacles, O Divinity.
वन्दे गुरूणां चरणारविन्दे संदर्शितस्वात्मसुखावबोधे ।
निःश्रेयसे जाङ्गलिकायमाने संसारहालाहलमोहशान्त्यै ॥

आबाहुपुरुषाकारं शङ्खचक्रासिधारिणम् ।
सहस्रशिरसं श्वेतं प्रणमामि पतञ्जलिम् ॥
vande gurūṇāṃ caraṇāravinde saṃdarśitasvātmasukhāvabodhe ।
niḥśreyase jāṅgalikāyamāne saṃsārahālāhalamohaśāntyai ॥

ābāhupuruṣākāraṃ śaṅkhacakrāsidhāriṇam ।
sahasraśirasaṃ śvetaṃ praṇamāmi patañjalim ॥
I adore the lotus feet of the Preceptors, by whom is revealed the knowledge of the bliss of the Self;
Who, like a physician of the wilderness, administer the remedy for the poison of worldly delusion, for the sake of ultimate beatitude.

Whose form is human down to the arms, bearing conch, discus and sword;
With a thousand white hoods, I bow down to Patañjali.
पूर्णमदः पूर्णमिदं पूर्णात्पूर्णमुदच्यते ।
पूर्णस्य पूर्णमादाय पूर्णमेवावशिष्यते ॥
pūrṇamadaḥ pūrṇamidaṃ pūrṇāt pūrṇamudacyate ।
pūrṇasya pūrṇamādāya pūrṇamevāvaśiṣyate ॥
That is full; this is full. From the full, the full doth proceed;
Taking the full from the full, the full itself remaineth.
स्वस्तिप्रजाभ्यः परिपालयन्तां न्यायेन मार्गेण महीं महीशाः ।
गोब्राह्मणेभ्यः शुभमस्तु नित्यं लोकाः समस्ताः सुखिनो भवन्तु ॥
svasti prajābhyaḥ paripālayantāṃ nyāyena mārgeṇa mahīṃ mahīśāḥ ।
go brāhmaṇebhyaḥ śubhamastu nityaṃ lokāḥ samastāḥ sukhino bhavantu ॥
May the rulers of the earth protect the people by the path of righteousness;
May there ever be prosperity for the cows and the wise, and may all beings in all worlds become happy.
मधु वाता ऋतायते मधु क्षरन्ति सिन्धवः ।
माध्वीर्नः सन्त्वोषधीः ॥
मधु नक्तमुतोषसो मधुमत्पार्थिवं रजः ।
मधु द्यौरस्तु नः पिता ॥
मधुमान्नो वनस्पतिर्मधुमाँ अस्तु सूर्यः ।
माध्वीर्गावो भवन्तु नः ॥
madhu vātā ṛtāyate madhu kṣaranti sindhavaḥ ।
mādhvīrnaḥ santvoṣadhīḥ ।
madhu naktamutoṣaso madhumatpārthivaṃ rajaḥ ।
madhu dyaurastu naḥ pitā ।
madhumān no vanaspatirmadhumām̐ astu sūryaḥ 。
mādhvīrgāvo bhavantu naḥ ॥
Sweet be the winds that blow unto us; sweet be the rivers that flow;
Sweet unto us be the herbs of healing.
Sweet be the night and sweet the dawns; sweet be the dust of the earth;
Sweet unto us be the heaven, our Father.
Sweet unto us be the forest tree; sweet be the Sun;
Sweet be our kine unto us.

Great Sayings

महावाक्य • mahāvākya • Great Sayings

असतो मा सद्गमय ।
तमसो मा ज्योतिर्गमय ।
मृत्योर्मा अमृतं गमय ।
asato mā sadgamaya ।
tamaso mā jyotirgamaya ।
mṛtyormā amṛtaṃ gamaya 。
From the unreal, lead me to the Real;
From darkness, lead me to Light;
From death, lead me to Immortality.
यो ब्रह्माणं विदधाति पूर्वं यो वै वेदांश्च प्रहिणोति तस्मै ।
तं ह देवमात्म बुद्धिप्रकाशं मुमुक्षुर्वै शरणमहं प्रपद्ये ॥
yo brahmāṇaṃ vidadhāti pūrvaṃ yo vai vedāṃśca prahiṇoti tasmai ।
taṃ ha devamātma buddhiprakāśaṃ mumukṣurvai śaraṇamahaṃ prapadye ॥
Unto that Divinity who in the beginning created Brahmā, and who delivered unto him the Vedas;
That God who is the Self, the light of the intellect, I, seeking liberation, do take refuge.

Peace Invocations

शान्तिप्रार्थना • śānti prārthanā • Peace Invocations

सर्वे भवन्तु सुखिनः सर्वे सन्तु निरामयाः ।
सर्वे भद्राणि पश्यन्तु मा कश्चिद्दुःखभाग्भवेत् ॥
sarve bhavantu sukhinaḥ sarve santu nirāmayāḥ ।
sarve bhadrāṇi paśyantu mā kaścit duḥkhabhāg bhavet ॥
May all be happy; may all be free from infirmity;
May all behold what is auspicious; may none suffer sorrow.
स्वस्ति प्रजाभ्यः परिपालयन्तां न्यायेन मार्गेण महीं महीशाः ।
गोब्राह्मणेभ्यश्शुभमस्तु नित्यं लोकास्समस्तास्सुखिनो भवन्तु ॥
svasti prajābhyaḥ paripālayantāṃ nyāyēna mārgēṇa mahīṃ mahīśāḥ ।
gōbrāhmaṇēbhyaśśubhamastu nityaṃ lōkāssamastāssukhinō bhavantu ॥
May the rulers of the earth protect the people by the path of righteousness;
May there ever be prosperity for the cows and the wise, and may all beings in all worlds become happy.
काले वर्षतु पर्जन्यः पृथिवी सस्यशालिनी ॥
देशोऽयं क्षोभरहितः ब्राह्मणास्सन्तु निर्भयाः ॥
kāle varṣatu parjanyaḥ pṛthivī sasyaśālinī ॥
deśo'yaṃ kṣobharahitaḥ brāhmaṇāssantu nirbhayāḥ ॥
May the rain-god send rain in due season; may the earth be rich with crops;
May this country be free from turmoil, and may the wise be free from fear.
सर्वेशां स्वस्तिर्भवतु । सर्वेशां शान्तिर्भवतु ॥
सर्वेशां पुर्णंभवतु । सर्वेशां मङ्गलंभवतु ॥
sarveśāṃ svastirbhavatu sarveśāṃ śāntirbhavatu ॥
sarveśāṃ purṇaṃbhavatu sarveśāṃ maṅgalaṃbhavatu ॥
May there be well-being for all; may there be peace for all;
May there be fullness for all; may there be prosperity for all.
द्यौः शान्तिरन्तरिक्षं शान्तिः
पृथिवी शान्तिरापः शान्तिरोषधयः शान्तिः ।
वनस्पतयः शान्तिर्विश्वेदेवाः शान्तिब्रह्म शान्तिः
सर्वं शान्तिः शान्तिरेव शान्तिः सा मा शान्तिरेधि ॥
dyauḥ śāntirantarikṣaṃ śāntiḥ
pṛthivī śāntirāpaḥ śāntiroṣadhayaḥ śāntiḥ ।
vanaspatayaḥ śāntirviśvedevāḥ śāntirbrahma śāntiḥ
sarvaṃ śāntiḥ śāntireva śāntiḥ sā mā śāntiredhi ॥
Peace be in the celestial region; peace be in the mid-region;
Peace be on earth; peace be in the waters; peace be in the herbs;
Peace be in the forest trees; peace be in the universal divinities; peace be in Brahman;
Peace be everywhere; peace indeed be peace; may that peace be established in me.

Reverence to the Preceptor

गुरुप्रणाम • guru praṇāma • Guru Reverence

आखण्डमण्डलाकारं व्याप्तं येन चराचरम् ।
तत्पदं दर्शितं येन तस्मै श्रीगुरवे नमः ॥१॥

अज्ञानतिमिरान्धस्य ज्ञानाञ्जनशालाकया ।
चक्षुरुन्मीलितं येन तस्मै श्रीगुरवे नमः ॥२॥

गुरुर्ब्रह्मा गुरुर्विष्णुर्गुरुर्देवो महेश्वरः ।
गुरुः साक्षात्परं ब्रह्म तस्मै श्रीगुरवे नमः ॥३॥
ākhaṇḍa-maṇḍalākāraṃ vyāptaṃ yena carācaram ।
tat-padaṃ darśitaṃ yena tasmai śrī-gurave namaḥ ॥1॥

ajñāna-timirāndhasya jñānāñjana-śālākayā ।
cakṣur-unmīlitaṃ yena tasmai śrī-gurave namaḥ ॥2॥

gurur-brahmā gurur-viṣṇur gurur-devo maheśvaraḥ 。
guruḥ sākṣāt paraṃ brahma tasmai śrī-gurave namaḥ ॥3॥
Unto that glorious Preceptor who hath revealed the state of That which pervadeth all the moving and unmoving, in the form of the undivided universal sphere, adoration.

Unto that glorious Preceptor who, with the applicator of the collyrium of knowledge, hath opened the eyes of one blinded by the darkness of ignorance, adoration.

The Preceptor is Brahmā; the Preceptor is Viṣṇu; the Preceptor is the great Lord Maheśvara;
The Preceptor is verily the Supreme Brahman; unto that glorious Preceptor, adoration.
नमोऽस्तु ते व्यास विशालबुद्धे फुल्लारविन्दायतपत्रनेत्र ।
येन त्वया भारततैलपूर्णः प्रज्वालितो ज्ञानमयः प्रदीपः ॥
namo'stu te vyāsa viśāla buddhe phullāravindāyata patra netra ।
yena tvayā bhārata taila pūrṇaḥ prajvālito jñāna mayaḥ pradīpaḥ ॥
Adoration unto thee, O Vyāsa, of vast intellect, whose eyes are like the petals of a full-blown lotus;
By whom the lamp of wisdom, filled with the oil of the Bhārata, hath been lighted.
सदाशिवसमारम्भां शङ्कराचार्यमध्यमाम् ।
अस्मदाचार्यपर्यन्तां वन्दे गुरुपरम्पराम् ॥
sadāśiva samārambhāṃ śaṅkarācārya madhyamām ।
asmadācārya paryantāṃ vande guru paramparām ॥
I salute the succession of Preceptors, beginning with Sadāśiva, having Śaṅkarācārya in the middle,
And extending up to my own Preceptor.
नारायणं पद्मभवं वशिष्ठं शक्तिं च तत्पुत्रपराशरं च ।
व्यासं शुकं गौडपदं महान्तं गोविन्दयोगीन्द्रमथास्य शिष्यम् ॥
nārāyaṇaṁ padmabhavaṁ vaśiṣṭhaṁ śaktiṁ ca tatputraparāśaraṁ ca ।
vyāsaṁ śukaṁ gauḍapadaṁ mahāntaṁ govindayogīndramathāsya śiṣyam ॥
Nārāyaṇa, the lotus-born, Vaśiṣṭha, Śakti and his son Parāśara,
Vyāsa, Śuka, the great Gauḍapāda, and Govinda-yogīndra, his disciple.
श्रीशंकराचार्यमथास्य पद्मपादं च हस्तामलकं च शिष्यम् ।
तं तोटकं वार्तिककारमन्यानस्मद् गुरून्संततमानतोऽस्मि ॥
śrī śaṁkarācārya mathāsya padmapādaṁ ca hastāmalakaṁ ca śiṣyam ।
taṁ toṭakaṁ vārtikakāra manyānasmad gurūn saṁtatamānato'smi ॥
Then Śrī Śaṅkarācārya, and his disciples Padmapāda and Hastāmalaka,
Toṭaka, the author of the Vārtika, and others; my Preceptors, I constantly bow down.
श्रुतिस्मृतिपुराणानामालयं करुणालयम् ।

I bow down to the blessed feet of Śaṅkara, the benefactor of the world.

सूत्रभाष्यकृतौ वन्दे भगवन्तौ पुनः पुनः ॥
śaṁkaraṁ śaṁkarācāryaṁ keśavaṁ bādarāyaṇam ।
sūtrabhāṣyakṛtau vande bhagavantau punaḥ punaḥ ॥
