THE YOGA SUTRAS

पतञ्जलि योग सूत्राणि

patañjali yōga sūtrāṇi

Invocation The Book of Union The Book of Practice The Book of Powers The Book of Freedom

Invocation

योगेन चित्तस्य पदेन वाचां ।

मलं शरीरस्य च वैद्यकेन ॥

योऽपाकरोत्तमं प्रवरं मुनीनां ।

पतञ्जलिं प्राञ्जलिरानतोऽस्मि ॥

yogena cittasya padena vācāṁ ।

malaṁ śarīrasya ca vaidyakena ॥

yo'pākarottamaṁ pravaraṁ munīnāṁ ।

patañjaliṁ prāñjalirānato'smi ॥

I bow with folded hands unto Patañjali,

Most excellent of sages, who bestowed

Through Yoga, purity of mind;

Through Grammar, purity of speech;

Through Medicine, purity of body.

आबाहुपुरुषाकारं शङ्खचक्रासिधारिणम् ।

सहस्रशिरसं श्वेतं प्रणमामि पतञ्जलिम् ॥

ābāhupuruṣākāraṃ śaṅkhacakrāsidhāriṇam ।

sahasraśirasaṃ śvetaṃ praṇamāmi patañjalim ॥

I prostrate before Patañjali,

White-complexioned, with thousand radiant heads,

In human form above the shoulders,

Bearing the conch, discus, and sword.

Chapter First: On Spiritual Union

समाधिपादः

samādhi pādaḥ

1.1
The Opening

अथ योगानुशासनम् ॥

atha yogānuśāsanam ॥

Here beginneth the instruction in Yoga.

1.2
Yoga Defined

योगश्चित्तवृत्तिनिरोधः ॥

yogaś citta vṛtti nirodhaḥ ॥

Yoga is the restraint of the modifications of the mind.

1.3
The Seer Established

तदा द्रष्टुः स्वरूपेऽवस्थानम् ॥

tadā draṣṭuḥ svarūpe'vasthānam ॥

Then the Seer abideth in His own nature.

1.4
Otherwise Identified

वृत्तिसारूप्यमितरत्र ॥

vṛtti sārūpyam itaratra ॥

At other times, assimilation with the modifications.

1.5
Fivefold Modifications

वृत्तयः पञ्चतय्यः क्लिष्टाऽक्लिष्टाः ॥

vṛttayaḥ pañcatayyaḥ kliṣṭā'kliṣṭāḥ ॥

The modifications are fivefold, painful and non-painful.

1.6
The Five Named

प्रमाणविपर्ययविकल्पनिद्रास्मृतयः ॥

pramāṇa viparyaya vikalpa nidrā smṛtayaḥ ॥

Right knowledge, error, imagination, sleep, and memory.

1.7
Sources of Right Knowledge

प्रत्यक्षानुमानागमाः प्रमाणानि ॥

pratyakṣānumānāgamāḥ pramāṇāni ॥

Direct perception, inference, and testimony are the sources of right knowledge.

1.8
Error Defined

विपर्ययो मिथ्याज्ञानमतद्रूपप्रतिष्ठम् ॥

viparyayo mithyājñānam atadrūpa pratiṣṭham ॥

Error is false knowledge, not founded on the thing's true nature.

1.9
Imagination's Nature

शब्दज्ञानानुपाती वस्तुशून्यो विकल्पः ॥

śabdajñānānupātī vastu śūnyo vikalpaḥ ॥

Imagination, following verbal knowledge, is devoid of substance.

1.10
Sleep's Support

अभावप्रत्ययालम्बना वृत्तिर्निद्रा ॥

abhāva pratyayālambanā vṛttir nidrā ॥

Sleep is a modification supported by the idea of absence.

1.11
Memory's Nature

अनुभूतविषयासम्प्रमोषः स्मृतिः ॥

anubhūta viṣayāsampramoṣaḥ smṛtiḥ ॥

Memory is the not letting slip away of experienced objects.

1.12
The Twofold Path

अभ्यासवैराग्याभ्यां तन्निरोधः ॥

abhyāsa vairāgyābhyāṃ tannirodhaḥ ॥

Their restraint is by practice and non-attachment.

1.13
Practice Explained

तत्र स्थितौ यत्नोऽभ्यासः ॥

tatra sthitau yatno'bhyāsaḥ ॥

Practice is the effort to be firm in that state.

1.14
Long Practice

स तु दीर्घकालनैरन्तर्यसत्कारासेवितो दृढभूमिः ॥

sa tu dīrgha kāla nairantarya satkārāsevito dṛḍhabhūmiḥ ॥

And that becomes firmly grounded when attended to for a long time, without interruption, with devotion.

1.15
Non-Attachment

दृष्टानुश्रविकविषयवितृष्णस्य वशीकारसञ्ज्ञा वैराग्यम् ॥

dṛṣṭānuśravika viṣaya vitṛṣṇasya vaśīkārasañjñā vairāgyam ॥

Non-attachment is the consciousness of self-mastery in him who is free from craving for seen or heard objects.

1.16
Highest Non-Attachment

तत्परं पुरुषख्यातेर्गुणवैतृष्ण्यम् ॥

tatparaṃ puruṣa khyāter guṇavaitṛṣṇyam ॥

That is the highest wherein from knowledge of the Spirit there cometh cessation of all desire for the qualities.

1.17
Union with Reasoning

वितर्क विचारानन्दास्मितारूपानुगमात् संप्रज्ञातः ॥

vitarka vicārānandāsmitārūpānugamāt saṃprajñātaḥ ॥

The cognitive union is accompanied by reasoning, reflection, bliss, and I-am-ness.

1.18
Union Beyond Cognition

विरामप्रत्ययाभ्यासपूर्वः संस्कारशेषोऽन्यः ॥

virāma pratyayābhyāsa pūrvaḥ saṃskāraśeṣo'nyaḥ ॥

The other union hath practice of cessation as its prerequisite; only latent impressions remain.

1.19
The Disembodied and Merged

भवप्रत्ययो विदेहप्रकृतिलयानाम् ॥

bhavapratyayo videha prakṛtilayānām ॥

The disembodied and the merged in nature have existence as their support.

1.20
The Path of Others

श्रद्धावीर्यस्मृतिसमाधिप्रज्ञापूर्वक इतरेषाम् ॥

śraddhā vīrya smṛti samādhiprajñā pūrvaka itareṣām ॥

For others, it is preceded by faith, energy, mindfulness, union, and discernment.

1.21
For the Zealous

तीव्रसंवेगानामासन्नः ॥

tīvrasaṃvegānāmāsannaḥ ॥

For those of intense ardour, the goal is near.

1.22
Degrees of Zeal

मृदुमध्याधिमात्रत्वात्ततोऽपि विशेषः ॥

mṛdu madhyādhimātratvāttato'pi viśeṣaḥ ॥

There is distinction according to mild, moderate, and intense practice.

1.23
Through Surrender

ईश्वरप्रणिधानाद्वा ॥

īśvarapraṇidhānādvā ॥

Or by surrender to the Lord.

1.24
The Lord's Nature

क्लेशकर्मविपाकाशयैरपरामृष्टः पुरुषविशेष ईश्वरः ॥

kleśa karma vipākāśayairaparāmṛṣṭaḥ puruṣaviśeṣa īśvaraḥ ॥

The Lord is a distinct Spirit, untouched by afflictions, actions, fruits, or impressions.

1.25
Infinite Knowledge

तत्र निरतिशयं सर्वज्ञबीजम् ॥

tatra niratiśayaṃ sarvajñabījam ॥

In Him is the unsurpassed seed of omniscience.

1.26
The First Teacher

स पूर्वेषामपि गुरुः कालेनानवच्छेदात् ॥

sa pūrveṣām api guruḥ kālenānavacchedāt ॥

He is the teacher of even the ancients, being untouched by time.

1.27
His Sacred Word

तस्य वाचकः प्रणवः ॥

tasya vācakaḥ praṇavaḥ ॥

His word is the sacred syllable OM.

1.28
Its Constant Repetition

तज्जपस्तदर्थभावनम् ॥

tajjapas tadartha bhāvanam ॥

Its repetition and meditation on its meaning should be done.

1.29
Inner Awareness

ततः प्रत्यक्चेतनाधिगमोऽप्यन्तरायाभावश्च ॥

tataḥ pratyak cetanādhigamo'pyantarāyābhāvaśca ॥

Thence comes the realization of the inner consciousness and the disappearance of obstacles.

1.30
The Nine Obstacles

व्याधि स्त्यान संशय प्रमादालस्याविरति भ्रान्ति दर्शनालब्धभूमिकत्वानवस्थितत्वानि चित्तविक्षेपास्तेंऽतरायाः ॥

vyādhi styāna saṃśaya pramādālasyāvirati bhrānti darśanālabdhabhūmikatvānavasthitatvāni cittavikṣepāsteṃ'tarāyāḥ ॥

Disease, languor, doubt, carelessness, laziness, sensuality, delusion, non-attainment of stages, and instability are the distractions of the mind, which are the obstacles.

1.31
Accompanying Sufferings

दुःख दौर्मनस्याङ्गमेजयत्व श्वासप्रश्वासा विक्षेपसहभुवः ॥

duḥkha daurmanasyāṅgamejayatva śvāsapraśvāsā vikṣepa sahabhuvaḥ ॥

Pain, despair, nervousness, and irregular breathing accompany the distractions.

1.32
The Single Truth

तत्प्रतिषेधार्थमेकतत्त्वाभ्यासः ॥

tat pratiṣedhārtham ekatattvābhyāsaḥ ॥

To prevent these, practice should be done on one truth.

1.33
Purifying the Heart

मैत्रीकरुणामुदितोपेक्षाणां सुखदुःखपुण्यापुण्य विषयाणाम् भावनातश्चित्तप्रसादनम् ॥

maitrī karuṇā muditopekṣāṇāṃ sukha duḥkha puṇyāpuṇya viṣayāṇām bhāvanātaś citta prasādanam ॥

The mind becomes purified by the cultivation of friendliness towards the happy, compassion for the unhappy, delight in the virtuous, and indifference towards the wicked.

1.34
By Breath Mastery

प्रच्छर्दनविधारणाभ्यां वा प्राणस्य ॥

pracchardana vidhāraṇābhyāṃ vā prāṇasya ॥

Or by the expiration and retention of breath.

1.35
Higher Perception

विषयवती वा प्रवृत्तिरुत्पन्ना मनसः स्थिति निबन्धिनी ॥

viṣayavatī vā pravṛttirutpannā manasaḥ sthiti nibandhinī ॥

Or an objective mental activity which arises and steadies the mind.

1.36
The Sorrowless Light

विशोका वा ज्योतिष्मती ॥

viśokā vā jyotiṣmatī ॥

Or the state of sorrowlessness and illumination.

1.37
The Detached Mind

वीतरागविषयं वा चित्तम् ॥

vītarāga viṣayaṃ vā cittam ॥

Or by meditating on the mind which is free from desires.

1.38
Dream Knowledge

स्वप्ननिद्राज्ञानालम्बनं वा ॥

svapna nidrā jñānālambanaṃ vā ॥

Or by taking as the object the knowledge in dreams and sleep.

1.39
On Any Chosen Object

यथाभिमतध्यानाद्वा ॥

yathābhimata dhyānād vā ॥

Or by meditation on anything one chooses that is elevating.

1.40
Mastery Over All

परमाणुपरममहत्त्वान्तोऽस्य वशीकारः ॥

paramāṇu parama mahattvānto'sya vaśīkāraḥ ॥

His mastery extends from the finest atom to the greatest magnitude.

1.41
Crystal-like Mind

क्षीणवृत्तेरभिजातस्येव मणेर्ग्रहीतृग्रहणग्राह्येषु तत्स्थतदञ्जनता समापत्तिः ॥

kṣīṇa vṛtter abhijātasyēva maṇer grahītṛ grahaṇa grāhyeṣu tatstha tadañjanatā samāpattiḥ ॥

When the modifications have been weakened, the mind becomes like a transparent crystal, taking the form of whatever object is placed before it, whether that object be the knower, the knowing, or the known.

1.42
Union with Reasoning

तत्र शब्दार्थज्ञानविकल्पैः सङ्कीर्णा सवितर्का समापत्तिः ॥

tatra śabdārtha jñāna vikalpaiḥ saṅkīrṇā savitarkā samāpattiḥ ॥

The union with reasoning is that which is mixed with word, meaning, and knowledge.

1.43
Union Beyond Reasoning

स्मृतिपरिशुद्धौ स्वरूपशून्येवार्थमात्रनिर्भासा निर्वितर्का ॥

smṛti pariśuddhau svarūpa śūnyevārtha mātranirbhāsā nirvitarkā ॥

When the memory is purified, the union beyond reasoning shines forth as the object alone, devoid of its own nature.

1.44
Subtle Unions

एतयैव सविचारा निर्विचारा च सूक्ष्मविषया व्याख्याता ॥

ētayaiva savicārā nirvicārā ca sūkṣmaviṣayā vyākhyātā ॥

By this process, the unions with reflection and without reflection, whose objects are subtle, are explained.

1.45
The Subtle Realm

सूक्ष्मविषयत्वं चालिङ्गपर्यवसानम् ॥

sūkṣma viṣayatvaṃ cāliṅga paryavasānam ॥

The subtle objects end with nature's undifferentiated ground.

1.46
Union with Seed

ता एव सबीजः समाधिः ॥

tā ēva sabījaḥ samādhiḥ ॥

These are the only kinds of union with seed.

1.47
Supreme Clarity

निर्विचारवैशाराद्येऽध्यात्मप्रसादः ॥

nirvicāra vaiśārādye'dhyātma prasādaḥ ॥

On attaining the utmost purity of the union beyond reflection, there is the dawning of spiritual light.

1.48
Truth-bearing Wisdom

ऋतम्भरा तत्र प्रज्ञा ॥

ṛtambharā tatra prajñā ॥

Therein is wisdom that carries the seal of truth.

1.49
Special Knowledge

श्रुतानुमान प्रज्ञाभ्यामन्यविषया विशेषार्थत्वात् ॥

śrutānumāna prajñābhyām anya viṣayā viśeṣārthatvāt ॥

That wisdom is different from that obtained by testimony or inference, because it has a particular object.

1.50
The Dominant Impression

तज्जः संस्कारोऽन्यसंस्कार प्रतिबन्धी ॥

tajjaḥ saṃskāro'nyasaṃskāra pratibandhī ॥

The latent impression born of that wisdom opposes all other impressions.

1.51
Seedless Union

तस्यापि निरोधे सर्वनिरोधान्निर्बीजः समाधिः ॥

tasyāpi nirodhe sarva nirodhān nirbījaḥ samādhiḥ ॥

Upon the cessation of that also, through the restraint of all, cometh the Seedless Union.

The Book of Practice

साधनपादः

sādhana pādaḥ

2.1
The Yoga of Action

तपः स्वाध्यायेश्वरप्रणिधानानि क्रियायोगः ॥

tapaḥ svādhyāyēśvarapraṇidhānāni kriyā yōgaḥ ॥

Austerity, sacred study, and surrender to the Lord constitute the yoga of action.

2.2
The Dual Purpose

समाधिभावनार्थः क्लेशतनूकरणार्थश्च ॥

samādhi bhāvanārthaḥ klēśa tanūkaraṇārthaś ca ॥

They are for cultivating meditation and for attenuating the afflictions.

2.3
The Five Afflictions

अविद्यास्मितारागद्वेषाभिनिवेशाः क्लेशाः ॥

avidyāsmitā rāga dvēṣābhinivēśāḥ klēśāḥ ॥

The afflictions are ignorance, egoism, attachment, aversion, and clinging to life.

2.4
Ignorance as Field

अविद्या क्षेत्रमुत्तरेषां प्रसुप्ततनुविच्छिन्नोदाराणाम् ॥

avidyā kṣētram uttarēṣāṃ prasupta tanu vicchinnōdārāṇām ॥

Ignorance is the field for the others, whether they be dormant, attenuated, interrupted, or active.

2.5
Ignorance Defined

अनित्याशुचिदुःखानात्मसु नित्यशुचिसुखात्मख्यातिरविद्या ॥

anityāśuci duḥkhānātmasu nitya śuci sukhātmakhyātir avidyā ॥

Ignorance is seeing the eternal in the ephemeral, the pure in the impure, bliss in suffering, and the self in the non-self.

2.6
Egoism Explained

दृग्दर्शनशक्त्योरेकात्मतेवास्मिता ॥

dṛg darśana śaktyōr ēkātmatēvāsmitā ॥

Egoism is the identification of the seer with the instrument of seeing.

2.7
Attachment's Nature

सुखानुशयी रागः ॥

sukhānuśayī rāgaḥ ॥

Attachment is that which dwells on pleasure.

2.8
Aversion's Nature

दुःखानुशयी द्वेषः ॥

duḥkhānuśayī dvēṣaḥ ॥

Aversion is that which dwells on pain.

2.9
Clinging to Life

स्वरसवाही विदुषोऽपि तथारूढोऽभिनिवेशः ॥

svarasavāhī viduṣō'pi tathā rūḍhō'bhinivēśaḥ ॥

Flowing by its own energy, established even in the wise, is the clinging to life.

2.10
The Subtle Afflictions

ते प्रतिप्रसवहेयाः सूक्ष्माः ॥

tē pratiprasavahēyāḥ sūkṣmāḥ ॥

The subtle afflictions are to be abandoned by resolution into their origin.

2.11
Meditation's Power

ध्यानहेयास्तद्वृत्तयः ॥

dhyānahēyāstadvṛttayaḥ ॥

Their modifications are to be rejected by meditation.

2.12
The Store of Karma

क्लेशमूलः कर्माशयो दृष्टादृष्टजन्मवेदनीयः ॥

klēśa mūlaḥ karmāśayō dṛṣṭādṛṣṭa janma vēdanīyaḥ ॥

The store of karma has its root in the afflictions and is experienced in seen and unseen births.

2.13
Karma's Fruition

सति मूले तद् विपाकोजात्यायुर्भोगाः ॥

sati mūlē tad vipākōjātyāyur bhōgāḥ ॥

So long as the root exists, there will be fruition in the form of species, life, and experience.

2.14
Pleasure and Pain

ते ह्लादपरितापफलाः पुण्यापुण्यहेतुत्वात् ॥

tē hlāda paritāpa phalāḥ puṇyāpuṇya hētutvāt ॥

They bear the fruits of pleasure and pain, caused by merit and demerit.

2.15
All is Suffering

परिणामतापसंस्कारदुःखैर्गुणवृत्ति विरोधाच्च दुःखमेव सर्वं विवेकिनः ॥

pariṇāma tāpa saṃskāra duḥkhair guṇa vṛttivirōdhāc ca duḥkham ēva sarvaṃ vivēkinaḥ ॥

To the discerning, all is suffering, due to the pains of change, anxiety, and impressions, and because of the conflict between the gunas.

2.16
Future Suffering

हेयं दुःखमनागतम् ॥

hēyaṃ duḥkham anāgatam ॥

The suffering which is yet to come may be avoided.

2.17
The Cause of Suffering

द्रष्टृदृ श्ययोः संयोगो हेयहेतुः॥

draṣṭṛ dṛśyayōḥ saṃyōgō hēya hētuḥ ॥

The cause of that which is to be avoided is the union of the seer and the seen.

2.18
The Nature of the Seen

प्रकाश क्रिया स्थितिशीलं भूतेन्द्रियात्मकं भोगापवर्गार्थं दृश्यम् ॥

prakāśa kriyā sthitiśīlaṃ bhūtēndriyātmakaṃ bhōgāpavargārthaṃ dṛśyam ॥

The seen is composed of the elements and the senses, has the nature of illumination, action, and inertia, and exists for the purpose of experience and liberation.

2.19
The Stages of Qualities

विशेषाविशेषलिङ्गमात्रालिङ्गानि गुणपर्वाणि ॥

viśēṣāviśēṣa liṅgamātrāliṅgāni guṇa parvāṇi ॥

The stages of the qualities are the particular, the particularized, the indicated, and the unmanifest.

2.20
The Seer's Nature

द्रष्टा दृशिमात्रः शुद्धोऽपि प्रत्ययानुपश्यः ॥

draṣṭā dṛśimātraḥ śuddhō'pi pratyayānupaśyaḥ ॥

The Seer is pure consciousness, yet he perceives through the mind.

2.21
The Seen's Essence

तदर्थ एव दृश्यस्यात्मा ॥

tadartha ēva dṛśyasyātmā ॥

The essence of the seen exists only for the sake of the Seer.

2.22
Though Ceased

कृतार्थं प्रति नष्टमप्यनष्टं तदन्यसाधारणत्वात् ॥

kṛtārthaṃ prati naṣṭam apyanaṣṭaṃ tad anya sādhāraṇatvāt ॥

Though ceasing to exist for him whose purpose is fulfilled, it has not ceased to exist for others, being common to them.

2.23
Union's Purpose

स्व स्वामि शक्त्योः स्वरूपोपलब्धिहेतुः संयोगः ॥

sva svāmi śaktyōḥ svarūpōpalabdhihētuḥ saṃyōgaḥ ॥

The union is for the purpose of realizing the true nature of both the possessor and the possessed.

2.24
Ignorance as Cause

तस्य हेतुरविद्या ॥

tasya hēturavidyā ॥

The cause of that union is ignorance.

2.25
Liberation's Path

तदभावात् संयोगाभावो हानं तद् दृशेः कैवल्यम् ॥

tad abhāvāt saṃyōgābhāvō hānaṃ tad dṛśēḥ kaivalyam ॥

With the absence of ignorance, there is absence of union; this is the liberation of the Seer.

2.26
Discriminative Knowledge

विवेकख्यातिरविप्लवा हानोपायः ॥

vivēkakhyātiraviplavā hānōpāyaḥ ॥

The means of destruction is unfluctuating discriminative knowledge.

2.27
Sevenfold Wisdom

तस्य सप्तधा प्रान्तभूमिः प्रज्ञा ॥

tasya saptadhā prāntabhūmiḥ prajñā ॥

His wisdom is sevenfold, reaching to the summit.

2.28
Through the Eight Limbs

योगाङ्गानुष्ठानादशुद्धिक्षये ज्ञानदीप्तिराविवेकख्यातेः ॥

yōgāṅgānuṣṭhānād aśuddhi kṣayē jñānadīptir āvivēkakhyātēḥ ॥

From the practice of the limbs of yoga, impurities are destroyed and the light of wisdom leads to discriminative discernment.

2.29
The Eight Limbs

यमनियमासनप्राणायामप्रत्याहारधारणाध्यानसमाधयोऽष्टावङ्गानि ॥

yama niyamāsana prāṇāyāma pratyāhāra dhāraṇā dhyāna samādhayō'ṣṭāvaṅgāni ॥

The eight limbs are: restraints, observances, posture, breath control, withdrawal, concentration, meditation, and absorption.

2.30
The Restraints

अहिंसासत्यास्तेयब्रह्मचर्यापरिग्रहा यमाः ॥

ahiṃsā satyāstēya brahmacaryāparigrahā yamāḥ ॥

The restraints are: non-violence, truthfulness, non-stealing, continence, and non-possessiveness.

2.31
The Great Vow

जातिदेशकालसमयानवच्छिन्नाः सार्वभौमा महाव्रतम् ॥

jāti dēśa kāla samayānavacchinnāḥ sārvabhaumā mahāvratam ॥

These, not conditioned by class, place, time, or circumstance, are the great vow.

2.32
The Observances

शौचसन्तोषतपः स्वाध्यायेश्वरप्रणिधानानि नियमाः ॥

śauca santōṣa tapaḥ svādhyāyēśvarapraṇidhānāni niyamāḥ ॥

The observances are: purity, contentment, austerity, self-study, and surrender to the Lord.

2.33
Counteracting Thoughts

वितर्कबाधने प्रतिपक्षभावनम् ॥

vitarka bādhanē pratipakṣa bhāvanam ॥

When disturbed by negative thoughts, opposite ones should be cultivated.

2.34
Negative Thoughts

वितर्का हिंसादयः कृतकारितानुमोदिता लोभक्रोधमोहपूर्वका मृदुमध्याधिमात्रा दुःखाज्ञानानन्तफला इति प्रतिपक्षभावनम् ॥

vitarkā hiṃsādayaḥ kṛta kāritānumōditā lōbha krōdha mōha pūrvakā mṛdu madhyādhimātrā duḥkhājñānānanta phalā iti pratipakṣa bhāvanam ॥

Negative thoughts of violence, whether done, caused, or approved, spring from greed, anger, or delusion, and may be mild, moderate, or intense; they result in endless suffering and ignorance; thus cultivate the opposite.

2.35
Non-violence Established

अहिंसाप्रतिष्ठायां तत्सन्निधौ वैरत्यागः ॥

ahiṃsā pratiṣṭhāyāṃ tat sannidhau vaira tyāgaḥ ॥

When non-violence is established, enmity is abandoned in his presence.

2.36
Truthfulness Established

सत्यप्रतिष्ठायां क्रियाफलाश्रयत्वम् ॥

satya pratiṣṭhāyāṃ kriyāphalāśrayatvam ॥

When truthfulness is established, the fruits of actions depend on him.

2.37
Non-stealing Established

अस्तेयप्रतिष्ठायां सर्वरत्नोपस्थानम् ॥

astēyapratiṣṭhāyāṃ sarvaratnōpasthānam ॥

When non-stealing is established, all jewels present themselves.

2.38
Continence Established

ब्रह्मचर्यप्रतिष्ठायां वीर्यलाभः ॥

brahmacarya pratiṣṭhāyāṃ vīryalābhaḥ ॥

When continence is established, vigor is gained.

2.39
Non-possessiveness Established

अपरिग्रहस्थैर्ये जन्मकथन्तासम्बोधः ॥

aparigraha sthairyē janmakathantā sambōdhaḥ ॥

When non-possessiveness is confirmed, knowledge of the how and why of existence arises.

2.40
Purity's Effect

शौचात् स्वाङ्गजुगुप्सा परैरसंसर्गः ॥

śaucāt svāṅgajugupsā parairasaṃsargaḥ ॥

From purity comes disgust for one's own body and non-contact with others.

2.41
Mind's Purification

सत्त्वशुद्धिसौमनस्यैकाग्य्रेन्द्रियजयात्मदर्शनयोग्यत्वानि च ॥

sattvaśuddhi saumanasyaikāgyrēndriyajayātmadarśana yōgyatvāni ca ॥

Moreover, come purification of the mind, cheerfulness, concentration, mastery over the senses, and fitness for Self-realization.

2.42
Contentment's Fruit

सन्तोषादनुत्तमःसुखलाभः ॥

santōṣādanuttamaḥ sukhalābhaḥ ॥

From contentment comes supreme happiness.

2.43
Austerity's Effect

कायेन्द्रियसिद्धिरशुद्धिक्षयात् तपसः ॥

kāyēndriya siddhiraśuddhikṣayāt tapasaḥ ॥

From austerity, through the destruction of impurities, comes perfection of the body and senses.

2.44
Self-study's Fruit

स्वाध्यायादिष्टदेवतासम्प्रयोगः ॥

svādhyāyādiṣṭadēvatāsamprayōgaḥ ॥

From self-study comes communion with the chosen deity.

2.45
Surrender's Fruit

समाधिसिद्धिरीश्वरप्रणिधानात् ॥

samādhisiddhir īśvarapraṇidhānāt ॥

From surrender to the Lord comes the attainment of samādhi.

2.46
Posture Defined

स्थिरसुखमासनम् ॥

sthira sukhamāsanam ॥

Posture should be steady and comfortable.

2.47
Posture Perfected

प्रयत्नशैथिल्यानन्तसमापत्तिभ्याम् ॥

prayatna śaithilyānanta samāpattibhyām ॥

By relaxation of effort and meditation on the infinite, posture is perfected.

2.48
Freedom from Dualities

ततो द्वन्द्वानभिघातः ॥

tatō dvandvānabhighātaḥ ॥

Then one is not disturbed by the dualities.

2.49
Breath Control

तस्मिन् सति श्वासप्रश्वासयोर्गतिविच्छेदः प्राणायामः ॥

tasmin sati śvāsapraśvāsayōrgativicchēdaḥ prāṇāyāmaḥ ॥

That being acquired, the control of inhalation and exhalation follows.

2.50
Types of Breath Control

बाह्याभ्यन्तरस्तम्भवृत्तिर्देशकालसंख्याभिः परिदृष्टो दीर्घसूक्ष्मः ॥

bāhyābhyantara stambhavṛttirdēśakāla saṃkhyābhiḥ paridṛṣṭō dīrghasūkṣmaḥ ॥

Its modifications are external, internal, or stationary, regulated by place, time, and number, and become long and subtle.

2.51
The Fourth Type

बाह्याभ्यन्तरविषयाक्षेपी चतुर्थः ॥

bāhyābhyantara viṣayākṣēpī caturthaḥ ॥

The fourth type transcends the external and internal spheres.

2.52
The Veil Destroyed

ततः क्षीयते प्रकाशावरणम् ॥

tataḥ kṣīyatē prakāśāvaraṇam ॥

Then the covering of the light is destroyed.

2.53
Mind Fit for Concentration

धारणासु च योग्यता मनसः ॥

dhāraṇāsu ca yōgyatā manasaḥ ॥

And the mind becomes fit for concentration.

2.54
Withdrawal of Senses

स्वविषयासम्प्रयोगे चित्तस्वरूपानुकार इवेन्द्रियाणां प्रत्याहारः ॥

svaviṣayāsamprayōgē cittasvarūpānukāra ivēndriyāṇāṃ pratyāhāraḥ ॥

Withdrawal is the imitation by the senses of the mind's nature, by disengaging from their objects.

2.55
Supreme Mastery

ततः परमा वश्यतेन्द्रियाणाम् ॥

tataḥ paramā vaśyatēndriyāṇām ॥

Then comes the supreme mastery over the senses.

The Book of Powers

विभूतिपादः

vibhūti pādaḥ

3.1
Concentration Defined

देशबन्धश्चित्तस्य धारणा ॥

dēśabandhaścittasya dhāraṇā ॥

Concentration is the binding of the mind to one place.

3.2
Meditation Defined

तत्र प्रत्ययैकतानता ध्यानम् ॥

tatra pratyayaikatānatā dhyānam ॥

Meditation is the uninterrupted flow of cognition toward that object.

3.3
Samādhi Defined

तदेवार्थमात्रनिर्भासं स्वरूपशून्यमिव समाधिः ॥

tadēvārthamātranirbhāsaṃ svarūpaśūnyamiva samādhiḥ ॥

When the object alone shines forth, as if devoid of its own form, that is samādhi.

3.4
The Three Together

त्रयमेकत्र संयमः ॥

trayamēkatra saṃyamaḥ ॥

These three together constitute constraint.

3.5
Mastery Through Constraint

तज्जयात् प्रज्ञालोकः ॥

tajjayāt prajñālōkaḥ ॥

From mastery of that comes the light of wisdom.

3.6
Application by Stages

तस्य भूमिषु विनियोगः ॥

tasya bhūmiṣu viniyōgaḥ ॥

Its application is by stages.

3.7
The Three Internal

त्रयमन्तरङ्गं पूर्वेभ्यः ॥

trayamantaraṅgaṃ pūrvēbhyaḥ ॥

These three are more internal than the preceding.

3.8
External to Seedless

तदपि बहिरङ्गं निर्बीजस्य ॥

tadapi bahiraṅgaṃ nirbījasya ॥

But even these are external to the seedless samādhi.

3.9
Transformation of Restraint

व्युत्थाननिरोधसंस्कारयोरभिभवप्रादुर्भावौ निरोधक्षणचित्तान्वयो निरोधपरिणामः ॥

vyutthānanirōdhasaṃskārayōrabhibhavaprādurbhāvau nirōdhakṣaṇacittānvayō nirōdhapariṇāmaḥ ॥

The transformation of restraint is the connection of the mind with the moment of restraint when impressions of emergence and restraint arise and subside.

3.10
Peaceful Flow

तस्य प्रशान्तवाहिता संस्कारात् ॥

tasya praśāntavāhitā saṃskārāt ॥

Its peaceful flow comes from impressions.

3.11
Transformation of Samādhi

सर्वार्थतैकाग्रातयोः क्षयोदयौ चित्तस्य समाधिपरिणामः ॥

sarvārthataikāgrātayōḥ kṣayōdayau cittasya samādhipariṇāmaḥ ॥

The transformation of samādhi is the dwindling of distraction and rise of one-pointedness.

3.12
Transformation of One-pointedness

ततः पुनः शान्तोदितौ तुल्यप्रत्ययौ चित्तस्यैकाग्रता परिणामः ॥

tataḥ punaḥ śāntōditau tulyapratyayau cittasyaikāgratā pariṇāmaḥ ॥

Then again, when the subsiding and rising thought-waves are equal, there is the transformation of one-pointedness.

3.13
Transformation of Characteristics

एतेन भूतेन्द्रियेषु धर्मलक्षणावस्थापरिणामा व्याख्याताः ॥

ētēna bhūtēndriyēṣu dharmalakṣaṇāvasthāpariṇāmā vyākhyātāḥ ॥

By this are explained the transformations of characteristics, properties, and conditions in the elements and senses.

3.14
The Substratum

शान्तोदिताव्यपदेश्यधर्मानुपाती धर्मी ॥

śāntōditāvyapadēśyadharmānupātī dharmī ॥

The substratum is that which follows the characteristics of the quiescent, the risen, and the unmanifest.

3.15
Sequence of Transformation

क्रमान्यत्वं परिणामान्यत्वे हेतुः ॥

kramānyatvaṃ pariṇāmānyatvē hētuḥ ॥

The difference in sequence is the cause of the difference in transformation.

3.16
Knowledge of Past and Future

परिणामत्रयसंयमादतीतानागतज्ञानम् ॥

pariṇāmatrayasaṃyamādatītānāgatajñānam ॥

By constraint on the three transformations comes knowledge of the past and future.

3.17
Knowledge of Sounds

शब्दार्थप्रत्ययानामितरेतराध्यासात् सङ्करस्तत्प्रविभागसंयमात् सर्वभूतरुतज्ञानम् ॥

śabdārthapratyayānāmitarētarādhyāsāt saṅkarastatpravibhāgasaṃyamāt sarvabhūtarutajñānam ॥

By constraint on the distinction between word, meaning, and knowledge, which are ordinarily confused, comes knowledge of the sounds of all beings.

3.18
Knowledge of Previous Births

संस्कारसाक्षात्करणात् पूर्वजातिज्ञानम् ॥

saṃskārasākṣātkaraṇāt pūrvajātijñānam ॥

By direct perception of impressions comes knowledge of previous births.

3.19
Knowledge of Others' Minds

प्रत्ययस्य परचित्तज्ञानम् ॥

pratyayasya paracittajñānam ॥

By constraint on notions comes knowledge of others' minds.

3.20
But Not Their Support

न च तत् सालम्बनं तस्याविषयीभूतत्वात् ॥

na ca tat sālambanaṃ tasyāviṣayībhūtatvāt ॥

But not its support, that being not the object.

3.21
Disappearance of Body

कायरूपसंयमात् तद्ग्राह्यशक्तिस्तम्भे चक्षुः प्रकाशासम्प्रयोगेऽन्तर्धानम् ॥

kāyarūpasaṃyamāt tadgrāhyaśaktistambhē cakṣuḥ prakāśāsamprayōgē'ntardhānam ॥

By constraint on the form of the body, its perceptibility being suspended and the contact between eye and light being broken, disappearance comes.

3.22
Knowledge of Death

सोपक्रमं निरुपक्रमं च कर्म तत्संयमादपरान्तज्ञानमरिष्टेभ्यो वा ॥

sōpakramaṃ nirupakramaṃ ca karma tatsaṃyamādaparāntajñānamariṣṭēbhyō vā ॥

By constraint on karma, which is quick and slow in fruition, comes knowledge of death, or by portents.

3.23
Friendliness and Other Powers

मैत्र्यादिषु बलानि ॥

maitryādiṣu balāni ॥

By constraint on friendliness and other virtues come powers.

3.24
Powers Like an Elephant's

बलेषु हस्तिबलादीनी ॥

balēṣu hastibalādīnī ॥

In the powers, such as the strength of an elephant.

3.25
Knowledge of the Subtle

प्रवृत्त्यालोकन्यासात् सूक्ष्मव्यवहितविप्रकृष्टज्ञानम् ॥

pravṛttyālōkanyāsāt sūkṣmavyavahitaviprakṛṣṭajñānam ॥

By projecting the light of the senses comes knowledge of the subtle, hidden, and distant.

3.26
Knowledge of the Universe

भुवनज्ञानं सूर्ये संयमात् ॥

bhuvanajñānaṃ sūryē saṃyamāt ॥

By constraint on the sun comes knowledge of the universe.

3.27
Knowledge of the Stars

चन्द्रे ताराव्यूहज्ञानम् ॥

candrē tārāvyūhajñānam ॥

By constraint on the moon comes knowledge of the stars' arrangement.

3.28
Knowledge of Movement

ध्रुवे तद्गतिज्ञानम् ॥

dhruvē tadgatijñānam ॥

By constraint on the pole-star comes knowledge of their movements.

3.29
Knowledge of the Body

नाभिचक्रे कायव्यूहज्ञानम् ॥

nābhicakrē kāyavyūhajñānam ॥

By constraint on the navel wheel comes knowledge of the body's system.

3.30
Cessation of Hunger and Thirst

कण्ठकूपे क्षुत्पिपासानिवृत्तिः ॥

kaṇṭhakūpē kṣutpipāsānivṛttiḥ ॥

By constraint on the pit of the throat comes cessation of hunger and thirst.

3.31
Steadiness

कूर्मनाड्यां स्थैर्यम् ॥

kūrmanāḍyāṃ sthairyam ॥

By constraint on the tortoise channel comes steadiness.

3.32
Vision of Siddhas

मूर्धज्योतिषि सिद्धदर्शनम् ॥

mūrdhajyōtiṣi siddhadarśanam ॥

By constraint on the light in the head comes vision of the perfected ones.

3.33
Knowledge Through Prātibha

प्रातिभाद्वा सर्वम् ॥

prātibhādvā sarvam ॥

Or through the light of intuition comes all knowledge.

3.34
Knowledge of the Mind

हृदये चित्तसंवित् ॥

hṛdayē cittasaṃvit ॥

By constraint on the heart comes knowledge of the mind.

3.35
Distinction Between Self and Not-Self

सत्त्वपुरुषयोरत्यन्तासङ्कीर्णयोः प्रत्ययाविशेषो भोगः परार्थत्वात् स्वार्थसंयमात् पुरुषज्ञानम् ॥

sattvapuruṣayōratyantāsaṅkīrṇayōḥ pratyayāviśēṣō bhōgaḥ parārthatvāt svārthasaṃyamāt puruṣajñānam ॥

Experience results from the non-distinction between the pure intellect and the Self. By constraint on the Self comes knowledge of the Self.

3.36
Supernormal Powers

ततः प्रातिभश्रावणवेदनादर्शास्वादवार्ता जायन्ते ॥

tataḥ prātibhaśrāvaṇavēdanādarśāsvādavārtā jāyantē ॥

From that arise intuitional hearing, touch, vision, taste, and smell.

3.37
Obstacles to Samādhi

ते समाधावुपसर्गाव्युत्थाने सिद्धयः ॥

tē samādhāvupasargāvyutthānē siddhayaḥ ॥

These are obstacles to samādhi, but powers in the worldly state.

3.38
Mastery Over Elements

बन्धकारणशैथिल्यात् प्रचारसंवेदनाच्च चित्तस्य परशरीरावेशः ॥

bandhakāraṇaśaithilyāt pracārasaṃvēdanāc ca cittasya paraśarīrāvēśaḥ ॥

By relaxation of the causes of bondage and by knowledge of the channels, the mind can enter another's body.

3.39
Mastery Over Udāna

उदानजयाज्जलपङ्ककण्टकादिष्वसङ्ग उत्क्रान्तिश्च ॥

udānajayājjalapaṅkakaṇṭakādiṣvasaṅga utkrāntiśca ॥

By mastery over the udāna breath, one becomes light and can walk on water, swamps, thorns, etc., and can leave the body at will.

3.40
Mastery Over Samāna

समानजयाज्ज्वलनम् ॥

samānajayājjvalanam ॥

By mastery over the samāna breath comes radiance.

3.41
Divine Hearing

श्रोत्राकाशयोः सम्बन्धसंयमात् दिव्यं श्रोत्रम् ॥

śrōtrākāśayōḥ sambandhasaṃyamāt divyaṃ śrōtram ॥

By constraint on the relation between ear and ether comes divine hearing.

3.42
Levitation

कायाकाशयोः सम्बन्धसंयमात् लघुतूलसमापत्तेश्च आकाशगमनम् ॥

kāyākāśayōḥ sambandhasaṃyamāt laghutūlasamāpattēśca ākāśagamanam ॥

By constraint on the relation between body and ether, and by identification with light things like cotton, comes passage through space.

3.43
The Great Disincarnate

बहिरकल्पिता वृत्तिर्महाविदेहा ततः प्रकाशावरणक्षयः ॥

bahirakalpitā vṛttirmahāvidēhā tataḥ prakāśāvaraṇakṣayaḥ ॥

The state of consciousness outside the body is the great disincarnate; from that, the covering of light is removed.

3.44
Mastery Over Elements

स्थूलस्वरूपसूक्ष्मान्वयार्थवत्त्वसंयमात् भूतजयः ॥

sthūlasvarūpasūkṣmānvayārthavattvasaṃyamāt bhūtajayaḥ ॥

By constraint on the gross, the essential, the subtle, the connective, and the purposeful, comes mastery over the elements.

3.45
Siddhis and Perfection

ततोऽणिमादिप्रादुर्भावः कायसम्पत् तद्धर्मानभिघातश्च ॥

tatō'ṇimādiprādurbhāvaḥ kāyasampat taddharmānabhighātaśca ॥

From that comes the manifestation of the powers like miniaturization, and the perfection of the body, and the non-obstruction of its functions.

3.46
Perfection of Body

रूपलावण्यबलवज्रसंहननत्वानि कायसम्पत् ॥

rūpalāvaṇyabalavajrasaṃhananatvāni kāyasampat ॥

Beauty, grace, strength, and adamantine hardness constitute bodily perfection.

3.47
Mastery Over Senses

ग्रहणस्वरूपास्मितान्वयार्थवत्त्वसंयमादिन्द्रियजयः ॥

grahaṇasvarūpāsmitānvayārthavattvasaṃyamādindriyajayaḥ ॥

By constraint on the grasping, the essential nature, the I-sense, the connectedness, and the purposefulness of the senses, comes mastery over them.

3.48
Swiftness of Mind

ततो मनोजवित्वं विकरणभावः प्रधानजयश्च ॥

tatō manōjavitvaṃ vikaraṇabhāvaḥ pradhānajayaśca ॥

From that comes swiftness of mind, independence of instruments, and mastery over the primal cause.

3.49
Knowledge of Self

सत्त्वपुरुषान्यताख्यातिमात्रस्य सर्वभावाधिष्ठातृत्वं सर्वज्ञातृत्वञ्च ॥

sattvapuruṣānyatākhyātimātrasya sarvabhāvādhiṣṭhātṛtvaṃ sarvajñātṛtvañca ॥

For one who recognizes the distinction between the intellect and the Self, comes sovereignty over all states of being and omniscience.

3.50
Non-attachment to Powers

तद्वैराग्यादपि दोषबीजक्षये कैवल्यम् ॥

tadvairāgyādapi dōṣabījakṣayē kaivalyam ॥

By non-attachment even to that, the seeds of bondage are destroyed and isolation follows.

3.51
Invitation from Celestials

स्थान्युपनिमन्त्रणे सङ्गस्मयाकरणं पुनरनिष्टप्रसङ्गात् ॥

sthānyupanimantraṇē saṅgasmayākaraṇaṃ punaraniṣṭaprasaṅgāt ॥

When invited by celestial beings, there should be no attachment or pride, lest evil arise again.

3.52
Knowledge of Moments

क्षणतत्क्रमयोः संयमाद्विवेकजं ज्ञानम् ॥

kṣaṇatatkramayōḥ saṃyamādvivēkajaṃ jñānam ॥

By constraint on moments and their sequence comes discriminative knowledge.

3.53
Knowledge of Similar Things

जातिलक्षणदेशैरन्यतानवच्छेदात् तुल्ययोस्ततः प्रतिपत्तिः ॥

jātilakṣaṇadēśairanyatānavacchēdāt tulyayōstataḥ pratipattiḥ ॥

From that comes the knowledge of two similar things which are not confused by difference of species, characteristics, or position.

3.54
Transcendental Knowledge

तारकं सर्वविषयं सर्वथाविषयमक्रमं चेति विवेकजं ज्ञानम् ॥

tārakaṃ sarvaviṣayaṃ sarvathāviṣayamakramaṃ cēti vivēkajaṃ jñānam ॥

The knowledge that is born of discrimination is transcendent, includes all objects, all conditions, and is beyond succession.

3.55
Supreme Liberation

सत्त्वपुरुषयोः शुद्धिसाम्ये कैवल्यम् ॥

sattvapuruṣayōḥ śuddhisāmyē kaivalyam ॥

When there is equality of purity between the intellect and the Self, isolation comes.

The Book of Liberation

कैवल्यपादः

kaivalya pādaḥ

4.1
Siddhis from Birth and Other Means

जन्मौषधिमन्त्रतपःसमाधिजाः सिद्धयः ॥

janmauṣadhimantratapaḥsamādhijāḥ siddhayaḥ ॥

Perfections are born of birth, herbs, mantras, austerity, and samādhi.

4.2
Transformation into Another Species

जात्यन्तरपरिणामः प्रकृत्यापूरात् ॥

jātyantarapariṇāmaḥ prakṛtyāpūrāt ॥

Transformation into another species occurs through the inflow of nature.

4.3
Incidental Cause

निमित्तमप्रयोजकं प्रकृतीनां वरणभेदस्तु ततः क्षेत्रिकवत् ॥

nimittamaprayojakaṃ prakṛtīnāṃ varaṇabhedastu tataḥ kṣetrikavat ॥

The incidental cause does not initiate the transformations of nature, but removes obstacles as a farmer removes obstructions to water.

4.4
Created Minds

निर्माणचित्तान्यस्मितामात्रात् ॥

nirmāṇacittānyasmitāmātrāt ॥

Created minds arise from the sense of I-am-ness alone.

4.5
One Mind Directing Many

प्रवृत्तिभेदे प्रयोजकं चित्तमेकमनेकेषाम् ॥

pravṛttibhede prayojakaṃ cittamekamanekeṣām ॥

Though the activities differ, the one mind is the director of many minds.

4.6
Meditation-born Minds

तत्र ध्यानजमनाशयम् ॥

tatra dhyānajamanāśayam ॥

Among these, the meditation-born mind is without impressions.

4.7
Karma of Yogis

कर्माशुक्लाकृष्णं योगिनः त्रिविधमितरेषाम् ॥

karmāśuklākṛṣṇaṃ yoginaḥ trividhamitareṣām ॥

The karma of yogis is neither black nor white; for others, it is of three kinds.

4.8
Manifestation of Latent Impressions

ततस्तद्विपाकानुगुणानामेवाभिव्यक्तिर्वासनानाम् ॥

tatastadvipākānuguṇānāmēvābhivyaktirvāsanānām ॥

From these, only those latent impressions manifest which are in conformity with the fruition of karma.

4.9
Continuity of Memory and Impressions

जाति देश काल व्यवहितानामप्यानन्तर्यं स्मृतिसंस्कारयोः एकरूपत्वात् ॥

jāti dēśa kāla vyavahitānāmapyānantaryaṃ smṛtisaṃskārayōḥ ēkarūpatvāt ॥

There is a continuity of memory and impressions even when separated by birth, place, and time, due to their similarity.

4.10
Beginninglessness of Desire to Live

तासामनादित्वं चाशिषो नित्यत्वात् ॥

tāsāmanāditvaṃ cāśiṣo nityatvāt ॥

They are beginningless because the desire to live is eternal.

4.11
Dependence on Cause and Effect

हेतुफलाश्रयालम्बनैः सङ्गृहीतत्वातेषामभावेतदभावः ॥

hētuphalāśrayālambanaiḥ saṅgṛhītatvātēṣāmabhāvētadabhāvaḥ ॥

Being held together by cause, effect, basis, and support, with the disappearance of these, they too disappear.

4.12
Reality of Past and Future

अतीतानागतं स्वरूपतोऽस्त्यध्वभेदाद्धर्माणाम् ॥

atītānāgataṃ svarūpatō'styadhvabhēdāddharmāṇām ॥

Past and future exist in reality, the properties of objects being different in different time phases.

4.13
Manifest and Subtle Properties

ते व्यक्तसूक्ष्मा गुणात्मानः ॥

tē vyaktasūkṣmā guṇātmānaḥ ॥

Whether manifest or subtle, they are composed of the three gunas.

4.14
Reality of Objects

परिणामैकत्वाद्वस्तुतत्त्वम् ॥

pariṇāmaikatvādvastutattvam ॥

The reality of the object is due to the uniformity of the transformation.

4.15
Different Paths Due to Different Minds

वस्तुसाम्ये चित्तभेदात्तयोर्विभक्तः पन्थाः ॥

vastusāmye cittabhedāttayōrvibhaktaḥ panthāḥ ॥

The object being the same, the difference in the minds perceiving it creates different paths.

4.16
Independence of Objects

न चैकचित्ततन्त्रं वस्तु तत्प्रमाणकं तदा किं स्यात् ॥

na caikacittatantraṃ vastu tatpramāṇakaṃ tadā kiṃ syāt ॥

Nor does an object depend on a single mind, for if it did, what would become of it when not cognized by that mind?

4.17
Known and Unknown Objects

तदुपरागापेक्षित्वाच्चित्तस्य वस्तु ज्ञाताज्ञातम् ॥

taduparāgāpēkṣitvāccittasya vastu jñātājñātam ॥

An object is known or unknown according to the coloring of the mind that perceives it.

4.18
The Knower is Always Known

सदा ज्ञाताश्चित्तवृत्तयः तत्प्रभोः पुरुषस्यापरिणामित्वात् ॥

sadā jñātāścittavṛttayaḥ tatprabhōḥ puruṣasyāpariṇāmitvāt ॥

The modifications of the mind are always known to its lord, due to the immutability of the Purusha.

4.19
The Mind is Not Self-luminous

न तत्स्वाभासं दृश्यत्वात् ॥

na tatsvābhāsaṃ dṛśyatvāt ॥

The mind is not self-luminous, for it is perceptible.

4.20
Cannot Perceive Both Simultaneously

एकसमये चोभयानवधारणम् ॥

ēkasamayē cōbhayānavadhāraṇam ॥

It cannot perceive both itself and the object simultaneously.

4.21
Regressus ad Infinitum

चित्तान्तरदृश्ये बुद्धिबुद्धेरतिप्रसङ्गः स्मृतिसङ्करश्च ॥

cittāntaradṛśyē buddhibuddhēratiprasaṅgaḥ smṛtisaṅkaraśca ॥

If one mind were perceived by another, there would be regressus ad infinitum and confusion of memory.

4.22
Consciousness Reflects in Mind

चितेरप्रतिसंक्रमायास्तदाकारापत्तौ स्वबुद्धिसंवेदनम् ॥

citērapratisaṃkramāyāstadākārāpattau svabuddhisaṃvedanam ॥

When the unchanging consciousness assumes the form of that mind, then the experience of one's own mind becomes possible.

4.23
Mind Colored by Seer and Seen

द्रष्टृदृश्योपरक्तं चित्तं सर्वार्थम् ॥

draṣṭṛdṛśyoparaktaṃ cittaṃ sarvārtham ॥

The mind, colored by both seer and seen, understands everything.

4.24
Mind with Numerous Impressions

तदसंख्येयवासनाभिश्चित्रमपि परार्थं संहत्यकारित्वात् ॥

tadasaṃkhyeyavāsanābhiścitramapi parārthaṃ saṃhatyakāritvāt ॥

Though variegated by innumerable impressions, the mind exists for another, for it acts in association.

4.25
Cessation of False Identification

विशेषदर्शिन आत्मभावभावनानिवृत्तिः ॥

viśeṣadarśina ātmabhāvabhāvanānivṛttiḥ ॥

For one who sees the distinction, the meditation on the separate self ceases.

4.26
Mind Inclined Toward Discrimination

तदा विवेकनिम्नं कैवल्यप्राग्भारं चित्तम् ॥

tadā vivēkanimnaṃ kaivalyaprāgbhāraṃ cittam ॥

Then the mind becomes inclined toward discrimination and gravitates toward liberation.

4.27
Breaches in Discrimination

तच्छिद्रेषु प्रत्ययान्तराणि संस्कारेभ्यः ॥

tacchidrēṣu pratyayāntarāṇi saṃskārēbhyaḥ ॥

In the breaches of discrimination, other thoughts arise from impressions.

4.28
Removal Like Afflictions

हानमेषां क्लेशवदुक्तम् ॥

hānamēṣāṃ klēśavaduktam ॥

Their removal is like that of the afflictions, as described before.

4.29
The Cloud of Dharma Samādhi

प्रसंख्यानेऽप्यकुसीदस्य सर्वथाविवेकख्यातेर्धर्ममेघः समाधिः ॥

prasaṃkhyāne'pyakusīdasya sarvathāvivēkakhyāterdharmamēghaḥ samādhiḥ ॥

For one who remains undistracted even in the highest discrimination, comes the cloud of virtue samādhi.

4.30
Cessation of Afflictions and Karma

ततः क्लेशकर्मनिवृत्तिः ॥

tataḥ klēśakarmanivṛttiḥ ॥

From that comes the cessation of afflictions and karma.

4.31
Infinite Knowledge

तदा सर्वावरणमलापेतस्य ज्ञानस्यानन्त्यात् ज्ञेयमल्पम् ॥

tadā sarvāvaraṇamalāpētasya jñānasyānantyāt jñēyamalpam ॥

Then, all coverings and impurities being removed, knowledge becomes infinite, and what remains to be known is little.

4.32
Completion of the Gunas' Purpose

ततः कृतार्थानां परिणामक्रमसमाप्तिर्गुणानाम् ॥

tataḥ kṛtārthānāṃ pariṇāmakramasamāptirguṇānām ॥

Then the gunas, having fulfilled their purpose, cease their sequential transformations.

4.33
Sequence Perceived at End of Transformation

क्षणप्रतियोगी परिणामापरान्तनिर्ग्राह्यः क्रमः ॥

kṣaṇapratiyōgī pariṇāmāparāntanirgrāhyaḥ kramaḥ ॥

Sequence is the succession of moments perceived at the final end of transformation.

4.34
Liberation

पुरुषार्थशून्यानां गुणानां प्रतिप्रसवः कैवल्यं स्वरूपप्रतिष्ठा वा चितिशक्तिरिति ॥

puruṣārthaśūnyānāṃ guṇānāṃ pratiprasavaḥ kaivalyaṃ svarūpapratiṣṭhā vā citiśaktiriti ॥

Liberation is the reversal of the gunas, now empty of purpose for the Purusha; it is the establishment of the power of consciousness in its own nature.
